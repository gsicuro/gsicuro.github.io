About me

I graduated in Lecce, Italy, at University of Salento in 2011. I wrote my thesis under the supervision of Rosario Antonio Leo (University of Salento) and Piergiulio Tempesta (Universidad Complutense de Madrid). I obtained my PhD at the University of Pisa in 2015, working on the Euclidean assignment problem under the supervision of Sergio Caracciolo (University of Milan & Scuola Normale Superiore di Pisa).

After a two-year postdoc in the Centro Brasileiro de Pesquisas Físicas in Rio de Janeiro, in the group of Constantino Tsallis, I have been post-doctoral researcher in Statistical Physics at Sapienza Università di Roma, in the group of Giorgio Parisi. I moved then to Paris, in the group of Florent Krzakala at the École normale supérieure in Paris, and then joined the École Polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne, in Switzerland, where I also collaborated with Lenka Zdeborová. I moved to King’s College in January 2021.

Projects

Random matching and assignment problems

The research activities of the group concentrate on the analysis and development of mathematical theories and models with which to describe the statics and dynamics of disordered (or “complex”) systems in physics, biology, financial markets, and computer science.
